PART 1 — GETTING STARTED
================================================================================

1.1  WHAT THIS SYSTEM DOES
---------------------------
This is a business accounting system accessible through a web browser. It helps
you record and track:

  • Money coming in   — sales, receipts, and customer payments
  • Money going out   — purchases, disbursements, and vendor payments
  • Tax records       — VAT and withholding tax
  • Financial reports — trial balance, income statement, balance sheet, and more

Some pages handle cash directly (no invoice needed first). Other pages manage the
full invoice-and-payment cycle. Reports pull everything together so you can check
the accuracy and health of your books at any time.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1.2  LOGIN AND REGISTER
------------------------
LOGIN PAGE
  • Enter your username and password to sign in.
  • After a successful login you are taken to the Dashboard.
  • After login the system loads only the pages your account is allowed to use.
  • If you forget your password, contact your administrator.

REGISTER PAGE
  • Use this page to create a new user account (only if registration is enabled
    by your administrator).
  • Fill in your name, email, and a password.
  • After registering you are usually redirected to the Login page to sign in.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1.3  DASHBOARD
--------------
The Dashboard is the home page you see immediately after login.

What it shows:
  • Key financial totals at a glance (cash balances, outstanding invoices, etc.)
  • Recent transactions and activity
  • Quick links to the main pages you use most often

The Dashboard gives you a snapshot of where the business stands right now without
having to run a full report. Think of it as the front page of your accounting
records.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1.4  SEARCH AND TOP-HEADER TOOLS
----------------------------------
There is a search box in the top header bar that is available on every page.

How to use it:
  Step 1.  Type a keyword (for example a customer name, reference number,
           or description).
  Step 2.  Select a start date and an end date for the search range.
  Step 3.  Press Enter or click the Search button.
  Step 4.  A list of matching results appears. Results can include:
             – Sales invoices
             – Collections (customer payments)
             – Receipts
             – Purchases
             – Disbursements
             – Adjustments
  Step 5.  Click any result to open that record's page directly.

The search is useful when you remember a reference number or name but are not
sure which module it belongs to.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1.5  USER ACCESS AND PERMISSIONS
----------------------------------
Not every user sees every page. The system checks your role and shows only the
pages and actions you are allowed to use.

  • If you do not see a page listed in the menu, you do not have permission
    for it. Ask your administrator to grant you access.
  • Some pages are visible but certain buttons (approve, delete, edit) may be
    hidden depending on your role.
  • Administrators manage these settings in the Access Control page (Part 2.2).

PART 2 — ADMINISTRATION PAGES

2.1  COMPANY MANAGEMENT
------------------------
PURPOSE:  Store and update your company's details. These details appear on
          printed documents such as invoices, receipts, and payments.

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • View current company settings (name, address, tax ID, logo, etc.)
  • Edit and save updated company information

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES AUTOMATICALLY:
  • Updated company details appear on all newly printed documents immediately.

TYPICAL USER:  Administrator or accounting manager.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2.2  ACCESS CONTROL
--------------------
PURPOSE:  Define which pages and actions each user role can access.

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • View existing access rules per role
  • Grant or remove access to specific pages and actions (create, edit, approve,
    delete, print)
  • Assign roles to users

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES AUTOMATICALLY:
  • Changes take effect the next time the affected user logs in or refreshes.

TYPICAL USER:  Administrators only.
NOTE:  Be careful when removing access — users will lose the ability to see or
       act on those pages immediately.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2.3  USER MANAGEMENT
---------------------
PURPOSE:  Create and manage user accounts for everyone who needs to use the
          system.

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • View the list of all user accounts
  • Create a new user account (name, email, password, role)
  • Edit an existing user's details or role
  • Deactivate or delete accounts that are no longer needed

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES AUTOMATICALLY:
  • When a new user is created, the system applies access rights based on their
    assigned role.
  • Changes to a user's role update their page access automatically.

TYPICAL USER:  Administrators or HR/IT staff.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2.4  CHARTS OF ACCOUNTS (COA)
-------------------------------
PURPOSE:  This is the master list of every financial account category used in the
          system. All accounting entries (journal entries) must be mapped to an
          account from this list.

Common account types:
  • Assets         — what the business owns (cash, receivables, inventory)
  • Liabilities    — what the business owes (payables, loans)
  • Equity         — owner's share of the business
  • Revenue        — income from sales and services
  • Expenses       — costs of running the business

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • View the full list of accounts with their codes and types
  • Add a new account (code, name, type, normal balance)
  • Edit an existing account's details
  • Deactivate accounts no longer in use

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES AUTOMATICALLY:
  • New accounts become available immediately for use in transactions and
    journal entries.
  • Account balances are updated in real time whenever transactions are posted.
  • Reports (Trial Balance, Income Statement, Balance Sheet) read directly from
    this account structure.

IMPORTANT:  Changing an account type after transactions have been posted can
            affect your reports. Consult your accountant before making changes.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2.5  PRODUCTS & SERVICES
-------------------------
PURPOSE:  Maintain the catalog of products and services your business sells or
          buys. These items appear as line items on invoices, purchase orders,
          and receipts.

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • View the full product/service list
  • Add a new product or service (name, code/SKU, description, unit price,
    VAT rate, linked account)
  • Edit prices, descriptions, or tax rates
  • Deactivate items no longer offered

INPUTS WHEN CREATING / EDITING:
  • Item name and code (SKU)
  • Description
  • Unit price (selling price)
  • VAT rate (if taxable)
  • Inventory account (if stock is tracked)

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES AUTOMATICALLY:
  • When you add a product to a sales invoice or purchase, the system pulls in
    the price, tax rate, and computes the line total automatically.
  • If inventory tracking is enabled, sales reduce stock quantities and purchases
    increase them.
  • Inventory account balances update in the General Ledger automatically.

CONNECTED DATA:
  • products table — item master records
  • Links to sales_items and purchase_items when used on documents

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2.6  PROFORMA ENTRIES
----------------------
PURPOSE:  Create draft invoices (estimates or quotations) for customers before
          issuing a final invoice. Proforma records do not affect the books.

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • Create a proforma (draft) invoice for a customer
  • Edit the proforma before conversion
  • Convert a proforma into a real Sales invoice
  • View and print proforma documents for customer approval

INPUTS WHEN CREATING:
  • Customer name
  • Date and reference
  • Line items (products/services, quantity, price)
  • Notes or terms

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES AUTOMATICALLY:
  • Proforma entries do NOT create journal entries or affect account balances
    until they are converted to a Sales invoice and approved.
  • After conversion, the document becomes a standard sales invoice (see Part 6.1)
    and follows the normal sales posting process.

CONNECTED DATA:
  • proforma table and proforma_items lines
  • On conversion, creates a sales record

PART 4 — TAX PAGES
================================================================================

4.1  VAT MANAGEMENT
--------------------
PURPOSE:  Manage VAT (Value Added Tax) settings and view VAT-related records
          across sales and purchases.

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • View VAT rates configured in the system
  • Add or update VAT rate settings
  • View VAT records applied to sales and purchase transactions
  • Generate summaries of VAT collected (output tax) and VAT paid (input tax)

HOW VAT WORKS IN THE SYSTEM:
  • When you add a product or service to a sales invoice, VAT is computed
    automatically from the rate set on the product.
  • Similarly, VAT on purchase invoices is computed per line.
  • VAT amounts post to the VAT Payable or VAT Creditable accounts in the
    General Ledger automatically.

USE THIS PAGE FOR:  Setting up correct VAT rates before you start creating
invoices. Incorrect VAT rates will require adjustment entries later.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

4.2  WITHHOLDING TAX
---------------------
PURPOSE:  Record and track withholding taxes deducted from supplier payments or
          collected from customers.

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • View withholding tax entries linked to purchases or payments
  • Add withholding tax details to a payment or purchase record
  • Generate summaries for withholding tax compliance reporting

HOW IT WORKS:
  • When you pay a vendor and are required to withhold a percentage of the
    payment as tax, you record it here.
  • The withheld amount is posted to a Withholding Tax Payable account in the
    General Ledger.
  • The vendor receives the net amount, and the withheld tax is remitted to the
    tax authority separately.

PART 5 — CASH TRANSACTIONS
================================================================================

These two pages handle cash directly. They do NOT require creating an invoice
first. Use them for immediate cash in / cash out situations.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

5.1  RECEIPTS
--------------
PURPOSE:  Record cash or check received by the business — without a sales invoice.
          Use for direct cash sales, miscellaneous income, donations, or any money
          received that does not go through the sales invoice cycle.

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • Create a new receipt
  • Edit a draft receipt before approval
  • Approve / post a receipt to the books
  • View the list of all receipts (filter by date or status)
  • Click a receipt to see full detail
  • Print or download the receipt as a PDF

INPUTS WHEN CREATING:
  • Receipt date
  • Customer (optional — leave blank for non-customer cash receipts)
  • Reference number
  • Payment method (cash or check)
  • Bank or cash account receiving the funds
  • Amount received
  • Remarks or notes
  • Line items (description, quantity, amount) if needed

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES AUTOMATICALLY:
  Posting effects (when approved):
    Debit   →  Cash / Bank account (increases your cash balance)
    Credit  →  Income / Revenue account (or AR if applied to a customer invoice)

  • Creates journal entry lines in the General Ledger
  • Updates the cash account balance available in Bank Reconciliation
  • Creates an audit trail entry showing who created and approved the receipt

CONNECTED DATA CREATED OR UPDATED:
  • receipt record with reference number and date
  • receipt_items lines for each item
  • journal_entries (posted when approved)
  • Customer balance if linked to a customer
  • Bank / cash account balance for reconciliation
  • attachments if you upload supporting files (e.g., scanned cash voucher)
  • audit_trail entry

KEY RULE:  Only approved / posted receipts affect the books. Draft receipts do
not appear in reports.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

5.2  DISBURSEMENTS
------------------
PURPOSE:  Record cash or check paid out by the business — without a purchase
          invoice. Use for petty cash payments, miscellaneous expenses, or any
          direct cash payment that does not go through the purchase invoice cycle.

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • Create a new disbursement
  • Edit a draft before approval
  • Approve / post to the books
  • View the list of all disbursements (filter by date, payee, or status)
  • Click any disbursement to see full detail
  • Print or download as a PDF

INPUTS WHEN CREATING:
  • Payment date
  • Payee / vendor name
  • Reference number
  • Payment method (cash or check)
  • Bank or cash account the funds are coming from
  • Amount paid
  • Expense category / account
  • Remarks or notes
  • Line items (description, expense account, amount) if needed

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES AUTOMATICALLY:
  Posting effects (when approved):
    Credit  →  Cash / Bank account (decreases your cash balance)
    Debit   →  Expense or Asset account (records what the money was spent on)

  • Creates journal entry lines in the General Ledger
  • Updates the cash account balance visible in Bank Reconciliation
  • Creates an audit trail entry

CONNECTED DATA CREATED OR UPDATED:
  • disbursement record and disbursement_items lines
  • journal_entries (posted when approved)
  • Bank / cash account balance for reconciliation
  • attachments if you upload supporting files (e.g., supplier receipt)
  • audit_trail entry

KEY RULE:  Disbursements are for direct cash payments. If you have a vendor
invoice that needs to go through the approval process, use the Purchase page
instead (Part 7.1).

PART 6 — SALES CYCLE
================================================================================

The Sales cycle has three related pages that work together:
  Sales → creates a customer invoice (an amount the customer owes you)
  Collections → records a payment received against that invoice
  Aging Receivables → shows which invoices are still unpaid and how old they are

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

6.1  SALES (INVOICES)
----------------------
PURPOSE:  Create and manage customer sales invoices. When you sell something to a
          customer on credit (they will pay later), you record it here first.

TYPICAL WORKFLOW:
  Step 1.  Create a new sales invoice (status: PREPARED / DRAFT)
  Step 2.  A supervisor or checker reviews it (status: CHECKED)
  Step 3.  An authorized user approves and posts it (status: APPROVED / POSTED)
  Step 4.  The customer pays → record in Collections (Part 6.2)

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • Create a new sales invoice
  • Edit a draft invoice (before approval)
  • Submit for checking / approval
  • Approve and post to the books
  • View the list of all sales invoices (filter by date, customer, or status)
  • Click any invoice to see full line-item detail
  • Print the customer copy or an internal copy as a PDF
  • Attach supporting files (e.g., signed delivery receipts)

INPUTS WHEN CREATING:
  • Customer name
  • Invoice date and due date
  • Line items: product/service, description, quantity, unit price
  • Discounts (if any)
  • VAT (applied automatically from the product rate; editable if needed)
  • Payment terms (e.g., 30 days)
  • Reference number or PO number
  • Notes or special instructions

WHAT THE SYSTEM COMPUTES AUTOMATICALLY:
  • Line totals = quantity × unit price
  • Discounts deducted per line
  • VAT computed on each taxable line
  • Grand total = subtotal + VAT − discounts

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES AUTOMATICALLY (ON APPROVAL / POSTING):
  Posting effects:
    Debit   →  Accounts Receivable (AR) — the customer now owes you this amount
    Credit  →  Revenue account — you have earned income
    Credit  →  VAT Payable account — if VAT applies

  • Creates AR record showing the customer's outstanding balance
  • Inventory reduced if stock tracking is enabled
  • Produces a printable invoice document (customer copy and internal copy)

CONNECTED DATA CREATED OR UPDATED:
  • sales or invoice record and sales_items lines
  • AR balance for the customer
  • journal_entries (Debit AR, Credit Revenue, Credit VAT Payable)
  • Inventory stock levels (if applicable)
  • attachments
  • audit_trail entry

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

6.2  COLLECTIONS (CUSTOMER PAYMENTS)
--------------------------------------
PURPOSE:  Record a payment received from a customer and apply it to one or more
          outstanding sales invoices. This clears the customer's debt.

TYPICAL WORKFLOW:
  Step 1.  Customer sends payment (cash, check, or bank transfer).
  Step 2.  Open the Collections page and create a new collection record.
  Step 3.  Select the customer and choose which invoices this payment settles.
  Step 4.  Save and approve — the invoice balances are reduced.

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • Create a new collection / payment receipt
  • Apply the payment to one or multiple outstanding invoices
  • Edit a draft collection before posting
  • View the list of all collections (filter by date or customer)
  • Click any collection to see applied invoices and detail
  • Print or download a payment acknowledgment / official receipt as a PDF

INPUTS WHEN CREATING:
  • Collection date
  • Customer name
  • Payment method (cash, check, bank transfer)
  • Bank or check details (check number, bank name, date)
  • Amount received
  • Invoice(s) this payment is applied to
  • Remarks

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES AUTOMATICALLY:
  Posting effects (when approved):
    Debit   →  Cash / Bank account (your cash goes up)
    Credit  →  Accounts Receivable (AR) — the customer's balance goes down

  • Marks the applied invoices as partially or fully paid
  • If the payment exceeds the invoice total, the excess becomes a credit balance
    or advance for the customer
  • Updates the customer's outstanding balance

CONNECTED DATA CREATED OR UPDATED:
  • collection or customer payment record
  • Links to the applied sales invoices
  • AR balance reduced for the customer
  • journal_entries (Debit Cash/Bank, Credit AR)
  • Customer outstanding invoice status updated
  • attachments (e.g., deposit slips, check scans)
  • audit_trail entry

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

6.3  AGING RECEIVABLES
-----------------------
PURPOSE:  A report-style page that shows all unpaid customer invoices grouped by
          how overdue they are. Use this to know which customers need to be
          followed up for payment.

HOW IT WORKS:
  The system takes all posted sales invoices that have not been fully collected
  and groups them into age buckets based on their due date. Typical groupings:
    • Current (not yet due)
    • 1–30 days overdue
    • 31–60 days overdue
    • 61–90 days overdue
    • Over 90 days overdue

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • View the aging summary by customer
  • Filter by date to see aging as of a specific day
  • Click a customer row to see the individual overdue invoices
  • Export or print for collection follow-up

USE THIS PAGE TO:
  • Prioritize collection calls
  • Identify customers with long-overdue balances
  • Support credit limit decisions

PART 7 — PURCHASE CYCLE
================================================================================

The Purchase cycle has three related pages that work together:
  Purchase → records a vendor invoice (an amount you owe the vendor)
  Purchase Orders → formal requests to a vendor before the invoice arrives
  Payments → records money paid to a vendor, clearing the debt

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

7.1  PURCHASE (VENDOR INVOICES)
---------------------------------
PURPOSE:  Record vendor invoices for goods or services received. Use this page
          when you have received a supplier invoice and need to record what you
          owe.

TYPICAL WORKFLOW:
  Step 1.  Receive a vendor invoice.
  Step 2.  Create a new purchase record (status: DRAFT / PREPARED).
  Step 3.  Reviewer checks the details (status: CHECKED).
  Step 4.  Authorized user approves and posts it (status: APPROVED / POSTED).
  Step 5.  When you pay the vendor → record in Payments (Part 7.3).

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • Create a new purchase / vendor invoice
  • Edit a draft before approval
  • Submit for checking / approval
  • Approve and post to the books
  • View the list of all purchase records (filter by date, vendor, or status)
  • Click any record to see full detail
  • Print or download as a PDF
  • Attach supporting files (e.g., supplier invoice scan)

INPUTS WHEN CREATING:
  • Vendor name
  • Invoice date and due date
  • Line items: description, quantity, unit price, expense / asset account
  • VAT (if applicable)
  • Reference number (vendor's invoice number)
  • Payment terms
  • Notes or remarks

WHAT THE SYSTEM COMPUTES AUTOMATICALLY:
  • Line totals = quantity × unit price
  • VAT computed per line if applicable
  • Grand total = subtotal + VAT

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES AUTOMATICALLY (ON APPROVAL / POSTING):
  Posting effects:
    Debit   →  Expense account or Inventory account (the cost is recorded)
    Credit  →  Accounts Payable (AP) — you now owe the vendor this amount

  • Creates AP record showing your outstanding balance with the vendor
  • Inventory increased if stock tracking is enabled
  • May trigger a payment request notification

CONNECTED DATA CREATED OR UPDATED:
  • purchase record and purchase_items lines
  • AP balance for the vendor
  • journal_entries (Debit Expense/Inventory, Credit AP)
  • Inventory stock levels (if applicable)
  • attachments
  • audit_trail entry

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

7.2  PURCHASE ORDERS (PO)
--------------------------
PURPOSE:  Create a formal purchase order (request to buy) before receiving the
          vendor's invoice. A PO documents what you intend to buy, at what price,
          and from whom.

WHEN TO USE:
  Use Purchase Orders when your business requires formal approval before ordering
  goods or services. The PO is sent to the vendor; when the goods arrive and the
  vendor invoice comes, you convert the PO to a Purchase record.

TYPICAL WORKFLOW:
  Step 1.  Create a Purchase Order and get it approved.
  Step 2.  Send PO to the vendor.
  Step 3.  Goods / services are received.
  Step 4.  Vendor invoice arrives → convert the PO to a Purchase invoice
           (or to a Disbursement for immediate cash payment).

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • Create a new PO
  • Edit before approval
  • Approve the PO
  • Convert an approved PO into a Purchase invoice or a Disbursement
  • View the list of POs and their statuses
  • Print or download the PO document
  • Attach supporting files

INPUTS WHEN CREATING:
  • Vendor name
  • Order date and expected delivery date
  • Line items: items to order, quantities, unit prices
  • Reference number
  • Notes or delivery instructions

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES AUTOMATICALLY:
  • Purchase Orders do NOT post to the General Ledger — they are not yet expenses.
  • When converted to a Purchase invoice, the PO details pre-fill the new
    purchase record so you do not need to re-enter everything.
  • When converted to a Disbursement, the PO details pre-fill the disbursement
    for immediate cash payment.
  • Each conversion and approval is recorded in the audit trail.

CONNECTED DATA CREATED OR UPDATED:
  • purchase_order record and item lines
  • Links to purchase or disbursement records when converted
  • attachments
  • audit_trail entry

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

7.3  PAYMENTS (VENDOR PAYMENTS)
---------------------------------
PURPOSE:  Record a payment made to a vendor and apply it to one or more
          outstanding purchase invoices. This reduces what you owe the vendor.

TYPICAL WORKFLOW:
  Step 1.  You are ready to pay a vendor.
  Step 2.  Open the Payments page and create a new payment record.
  Step 3.  Select the vendor and choose which purchase invoice(s) this payment
           covers.
  Step 4.  Save and approve — the AP balance is reduced.

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • Create a new vendor payment record
  • Apply the payment to one or multiple outstanding purchase invoices
  • Edit a draft before posting
  • View the list of all payments (filter by date or vendor)
  • Click any payment to see applied invoices and full detail
  • Print or download a payment voucher or remittance slip as a PDF

INPUTS WHEN CREATING:
  • Payment date
  • Vendor name
  • Payment method (check, bank transfer, cash)
  • Bank or check details (check number, bank, date)
  • Amount paid
  • Purchase invoice(s) this payment is applied to
  • Bank charges or discounts (if any)
  • Remarks

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES AUTOMATICALLY:
  Posting effects (when approved):
    Debit   →  Accounts Payable (AP) — your balance owed to vendor goes down
    Credit  →  Cash / Bank account — your cash goes down

  • Marks the applied purchase invoices as partially or fully paid
  • Bank charges are posted to the relevant expense account if entered
  • Updates the vendor's outstanding AP balance

CONNECTED DATA CREATED OR UPDATED:
  • payment record linked to vendor invoices
  • AP balance reduced for the vendor
  • journal_entries (Debit AP, Credit Cash/Bank)
  • Vendor outstanding invoice status updated
  • attachments (e.g., bank transfer confirmation)
  • audit_trail entry

8.1  ADJUSTMENTS
-----------------
PURPOSE:  Make manual corrections or special accounting entries that do not
          belong to a standard sales, purchase, receipt, or disbursement. Use
          this page for accruals, reclassifications, corrections, and
          period-end entries.

COMMON USES:
  • Correct a wrong account that was used on a posted entry
  • Record an accrual (an expense or income that belongs in this period but
    has not yet been invoiced)
  • Reclassify an amount from one account to another
  • Record depreciation or amortization entries
  • Reverse a prior period entry

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • Create a new adjustment entry
  • Specify any combination of debit and credit accounts and amounts
  • Add explanatory notes for each entry
  • Edit a draft adjustment before approval
  • Approve / post to the books
  • View the list of all adjustments
  • Print or download adjustment vouchers
  • Attach supporting documentation

INPUTS WHEN CREATING:
  • Date of the adjustment
  • Reference number
  • Account to be debited (and amount)
  • Account to be credited (and amount)
  • Explanation or notes (required — always explain why you are making
    an adjustment)

IMPORTANT RULE:  Total debits must equal total credits. The system will not allow
you to post an unbalanced entry.

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES AUTOMATICALLY:
  • Posts the manual journal entry directly to the General Ledger
  • Account balances are updated immediately
  • The entry appears in the Trial Balance, General Ledger, and all reports

CONNECTED DATA CREATED OR UPDATED:
  • adjustment record
  • direct journal_entries in the GL
  • attachments
  • audit_trail entry

8.2  ADVANCES (PREPAYMENTS)
-----------------------------
PURPOSE:  Record money paid in advance — before a purchase or service is received
          — or money received from a customer before you have invoiced them.
          Advances are temporary balances that are later applied to the proper
          invoice or expense.

COMMON USES:
  • Down payment to a vendor before goods are delivered
  • Customer deposit received before a project starts
  • Employee cash advance for a business trip

TYPICAL WORKFLOW (Vendor Advance):
  Step 1.  Pay the vendor a deposit → record as an Advance (cash goes out,
           Advances/Prepaid asset goes up).
  Step 2.  Goods are received and the vendor invoice arrives → create a Purchase.
  Step 3.  Apply the advance to the purchase invoice → the Advances account goes
           down, AP goes down, or the cash owed to the vendor is reduced.

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • Create a new advance record
  • Edit a draft advance
  • Apply / settle an advance against a purchase or sales invoice
  • View the list of outstanding advances
  • Attach supporting files

INPUTS WHEN CREATING:
  • Date
  • Payee or customer name
  • Purpose of the advance
  • Amount
  • Related document or reference (optional)
  • Status

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES AUTOMATICALLY:
  On initial advance payment:
    Debit   →  Advances / Prepaid account (asset — money is now owed to you)
    Credit  →  Cash / Bank account

  When applied / settled:
    Debit   →  Expense or Inventory account (the actual cost is now recorded)
    Credit  →  Advances / Prepaid account (the prepaid balance is removed)

CONNECTED DATA CREATED OR UPDATED:
  • advance record
  • journal_entries for initial payment and for settlement
  • Links to purchase or sales records when applied
  • attachments
  • audit_trail entry

PART 9 — REPORTS
================================================================================

Reports pull data from posted journal entries and present it in standard
accounting formats. IMPORTANT: Only approved / posted transactions appear in
reports. Drafts are excluded.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

9.1  TRIAL BALANCE
-------------------
PURPOSE:  A fundamental accuracy check. Shows the balance of every account in
          the system for a selected period. Total debits must equal total credits.

HOW TO READ IT:
  • Every account in the Chart of Accounts appears as a row.
  • The Debit column shows total debits posted to that account.
  • The Credit column shows total credits posted to that account.
  • The bottom totals must match — if they don't, there is an error somewhere.

DATA SOURCE:
  Aggregates posted journal_entries joined with charts_of_accounts.
  Only POSTED entries (not drafts) are included.

HOW TO USE IT:
  Step 1.  Select a date range.
  Step 2.  Run the report.
  Step 3.  Check that the total Debit line equals the total Credit line.
  Step 4.  If they do not match, investigate for missing entries, unposted
           records, or adjustment errors.

USEFUL FOR:  Month-end and year-end closing checks before preparing financial
statements.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

9.2  INCOME STATEMENT (PROFIT & LOSS)
---------------------------------------
PURPOSE:  Shows whether the business made a profit or a loss during a selected
          period. Compares revenue earned against expenses incurred.

STRUCTURE:
  Revenue                              xxx
  Less: Cost of Sales                  (xx)
  Gross Profit                         xxx
  Less: Operating Expenses             (xx)
  Net Income / (Loss)                  xxx

DATA SOURCE:
  journal_entries filtered by account types Revenue and Expenses from the COA.

HOW THE SYSTEM COMPUTES IT:
  • Revenue = sum of credits minus debits on Revenue-type accounts
  • Expenses = sum of debits minus credits on Expense-type accounts
  • Net Income = Revenue − Expenses

HOW TO USE IT:
  • Select a start date and end date (usually a month or the full financial year).
  • Run the report to see revenue, expenses, and net income for that period.
  • Compare periods to track business performance over time.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

9.3  GENERAL LEDGER
--------------------
PURPOSE:  The detailed record of every posted transaction, organized by account.
          Think of it as the complete transaction diary for each account.

DATA SOURCE:
  journal_entries with full posting information (date, source document,
  amount, debit/credit type).

HOW IT WORKS:
  • Select an account (or all accounts) and a date range.
  • The report lists every journal line that touched that account during the
    period, with the source document reference.
  • Running balances are shown so you can trace how an account moved over time.
  • You can drill into any line to open the original source document (sales
    invoice, receipt, disbursement, etc.)

USEFUL FOR:  Detailed account analysis, investigating unusual balances,
reconciling specific accounts, and supporting audit inquiries.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

9.4  BALANCE SHEET
-------------------
PURPOSE:  A snapshot of the business's financial position on a specific date.
          Shows what the business owns (assets), what it owes (liabilities),
          and the owner's share (equity).

STRUCTURE:
  Assets
    Current Assets: Cash, AR, Inventory        xxx
    Non-Current Assets: Equipment, Property    xxx
  Total Assets                                 xxx

  Liabilities
    Current Liabilities: AP, VAT Payable       xxx
    Long-Term Liabilities: Loans               xxx
  Total Liabilities                            xxx

  Equity
    Owner's Capital and Retained Earnings      xxx
  Total Liabilities + Equity                   xxx
  (must equal Total Assets)

DATA SOURCE:
  Ending balances from journal_entries aggregated by COA account types
  (Assets, Liabilities, Equity) as of the selected date.

HOW TO USE IT:
  • Select a "as of" date (e.g., the last day of the month).
  • The system uses all GL balances up to and including that date.
  • Total Assets must equal Total Liabilities plus Equity. If not, investigate.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

9.5  STATEMENT OF COMPREHENSIVE INCOME
----------------------------------------
PURPOSE:  An extended version of the Income Statement that includes not only
          operating profit or loss but also other comprehensive income (OCI)
          items — items that affect equity but are not part of normal operations.

COMMON OCI ITEMS:
  • Unrealized gains or losses on certain investments
  • Foreign currency translation adjustments
  • Revaluation surplus on property

DATA SOURCE:
  Combination of revenue / expense GL balances and special OCI accounts from
  the Chart of Accounts.

HOW IT WORKS:
  Net Income (from P&L)                   xxx
  + Other Comprehensive Income items      xxx
  = Total Comprehensive Income            xxx

USEFUL FOR:  Businesses that follow full IFRS / PFRS reporting requirements.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

9.6  JOURNAL ENTRIES
---------------------
PURPOSE:  View every journal entry posted to the system. Useful for accountants,
          auditors, or anyone who needs to verify the raw accounting data.

DATA SOURCE:
  journal_entries table directly — all posted and manually created entries.

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • View the full list of journal entries in date order
  • Filter by date range, account, or reference number
  • Click any entry to see the full debit / credit detail and source document
  • Export for external use or audit submission

HOW IT DIFFERS FROM THE GL:
  • General Ledger (Part 9.3) groups by account and shows running balances.
  • Journal Entries shows each complete entry (all lines together) in
    chronological order.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

9.7  BANK RECONCILIATION
-------------------------
PURPOSE:  Compare your bank statement to your accounting books. The goal is to
          confirm that every transaction in the bank is also recorded in the
          system, and vice versa.

WHY IT MATTERS:
  Bank statements and your books can differ because of timing (checks not yet
  cleared, deposits in transit) or errors. Reconciling regularly catches
  mistakes early and ensures your cash balance is accurate.

HOW IT WORKS:
  Step 1.  Upload or enter the bank statement transactions for the period.
  Step 2.  The system matches bank transactions against your GL cash entries
           (Receipts, Collections, Payments, Disbursements).
  Step 3.  Mark transactions as "cleared" when they appear on both sides.
  Step 4.  Outstanding items (in the books but not on the bank statement, or
           vice versa) are listed separately.
  Step 5.  Create reconciling adjustment entries for any genuine differences
           (bank charges, interest, errors).
  Step 6.  Finalize the reconciliation when GL balance matches bank balance.

DATA SOURCE:
  Cash account GL transactions (Receipts, Collections, Payments, Disbursements)
  and user-provided bank statement lines.

CONNECTED DATA:
  • bank_reconciliation records
  • Links to journal_entries and bank statement items
  • Reconciling adjustment entries

9.8  AUDIT TRAIL

DATA SOURCE:

WHAT YOU CAN DO:
  • View the audit log
  • Filter by user, date, page, or record
  • See the before and after values for changes (where recorded)
  • Export for compliance or investigation purposes

USEFUL FOR:
  • Investigating discrepancies ("who changed this entry?")
  • Compliance with internal controls
  • External audits
  • Detecting unauthorized changes

10.1  KEY TRANSACTION FLOWS EXPLAINED SIMPLY
---------------------------------------------

  CASH IN (no invoice):
    Customer or other party pays you cash directly.
    → Use RECEIPTS (Part 5.1)
    → Effect: Cash goes up, Income is recorded

  CASH OUT (no invoice):
    You pay something in cash directly (petty cash, minor expenses).
    → Use DISBURSEMENTS (Part 5.2)
    → Effect: Cash goes down, Expense is recorded

  CREDIT SALE (invoice first, payment later):
    You sell to a customer who will pay you in the future.
    → Step 1: Create SALES invoice (Part 6.1)   → AR goes up, Revenue recorded
    → Step 2: Customer pays → COLLECTIONS (Part 6.2) → Cash up, AR goes down

  CREDIT PURCHASE (invoice received, payment later):
    You receive goods or services and will pay the vendor later.
    → Step 1: Create PURCHASE invoice (Part 7.1) → Expense recorded, AP goes up
    → Step 2: You pay vendor → PAYMENTS (Part 7.3) → AP goes down, Cash down

  PURCHASE ORDER (request before ordering):
    You need approval before ordering from a vendor.
    → Step 1: Create PURCHASE ORDER (Part 7.2) → No ledger effect
    → Step 2: Receive goods, get invoice → Convert PO to PURCHASE (Part 7.1)
    → Step 3: Pay vendor → PAYMENTS (Part 7.3)

  CORRECTIONS:
    Something was posted incorrectly.
    → Use ADJUSTMENTS (Part 8.1)
    → Effect: Manual journal entry directly to GL

  PREPAYMENTS:
    You or a customer pays before the invoice is issued.
    → Use ADVANCES (Part 8.2)
    → Effect: Prepaid / Advance account records the money; settled when invoice
      arrives

10.4  AUTOMATION AND POSTING FLOW (SUMMARY)
---------------------------------------------
The system automates the accounting work so you only need to enter the business
event. Here is what happens behind the scenes:

  When you CREATE A SALES INVOICE (and approve it):
    → AR record created (customer owes you)
    → Journal: Debit AR / Credit Revenue / Credit VAT Payable
    → Inventory may be reduced

  When you RECORD A COLLECTION:
    → Journal: Debit Cash / Credit AR
    → Customer's outstanding balance decreases
    → Invoice marked as paid

  When you CREATE A PURCHASE INVOICE (and approve it):
    → AP record created (you owe the vendor)
    → Journal: Debit Expense/Inventory / Credit AP
    → Inventory may be increased

  When you RECORD A PAYMENT TO A VENDOR:
    → Journal: Debit AP / Credit Cash
    → Vendor's outstanding balance decreases
    → Purchase invoice marked as paid

  When you CREATE A RECEIPT:
    → Journal: Debit Cash / Credit Income
    → No AR/AP lifecycle — direct cash posting

  When you CREATE A DISBURSEMENT:
    → Journal: Debit Expense / Credit Cash
    → No AR/AP lifecycle — direct cash posting

  When you POST AN ADJUSTMENT:
    → Manual journal entry goes directly to GL
    → Account balances updated immediately

10.5  GENERAL TIPS FOR USERS
------------------------------
  1.  ALWAYS APPROVE BEFORE RUNNING REPORTS
      Draft records are not included in any report. Make sure all records are
      approved / posted before checking your Trial Balance or financial
      statements.

  2.  USE TRIAL BALANCE FIRST
      Before preparing month-end reports, run the Trial Balance to check that
      debits equal credits. An out-of-balance Trial Balance means there is a
      data entry or posting error somewhere.

  3.  RECONCILE REGULARLY
      Run Bank Reconciliation at least once a month. This catches missing entries,
      bank errors, and timing differences before they become hard to trace.

  4.  ALWAYS ADD NOTES TO ADJUSTMENTS
      The Notes field on Adjustments is very important. In 6 months, you (or
      your auditor) will need to understand why the entry was made. Always write
      a clear explanation.

  5.  PROFORMAS DO NOT AFFECT THE BOOKS
      Proforma entries and Purchase Orders are planning documents. They do not
      post to the General Ledger. Only converted and approved documents affect
      your accounts.

  6.  KNOW YOUR ROLE
      If you cannot see a page or a button, your role does not have permission
      for it. Ask your administrator — do not try to work around it.

  7.  DATES MATTER
      Always enter the correct transaction date, not today's date, unless
      today is the actual transaction date. Reports are date-based; wrong dates
      put transactions in the wrong reporting period.

  8.  PDF DOWNLOADS ARE FINAL COPIES
      Downloaded PDFs reflect the data at the time of download. If you edit a
      record after downloading, download a fresh copy.

  9.  THE AUDIT TRAIL RECORDS EVERYTHING
      Every action you take in the system is logged. This is for your protection
      as much as for controls. Always work accurately and honestly.

  10. WHEN IN DOUBT, ASK
      If you are unsure whether to use Receipts or Collections, Disbursements or
      Payments, or any other module — ask your supervisor or accountant before
      entering data. A wrong posting is harder to fix than an un-posted draft.
